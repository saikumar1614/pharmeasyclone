What is LASIK surgery?

LASIK is a term that means laser in-situ keratomileusis. It is a type of eye surgery that is used to correct defective vision in people. LASIK surgery is a laser refractive surgery that uses a special kind of laser to change the shape in front of the cornea (the outer layer of the eye that helps light refract to the lenses).

Symptoms of Eye Disorders
  • Difficulty in viewing objects
  • Red eyes
  • Night blindness
  • Headaches
  • Sensitivity to light
  • Dry eyes
  • Excessive tearing
  • Blurred vision
Diagnosis of Refractive Disorders

An eye specialist (opthalmologist) diagnoses refractive disorders in your eyes with the help of certain diagnostic tests. These tests may include:

  • Visual acuity test - You are made to read a vision chart. The doctor then tries on different lenses to determine where your vision needs correction.
  • Retinoscopy - This test helps determine the refractive errors in the eye. It is mandatory before a LASIK procedure.
  • Corneal topography - This is a computer test that analyses any disorders in the eye.
  • Tonometry - This diagnostic test checks for eye pressure.
  • The ophthalmologist may also perform tests to check for dryness in the eyes.
Refractive Correction Treatment Options

Here are the types of refractive correction surgeries that the doctor might suggest to you:

  • LASIK
    1. 1) Blade - For the blade LASIK surgery , a surgeon uses a fine blade to open the flap of the cornea.
    2. 2) Bladeless - The bladeless LASIK surgery is done using a laser to cut open the flap of the cornea. This surgery is known as the FEMTO LASIK and is a painless surgery.
  • Photorefractive Keratectomy (PRK)
    This surgery also uses a laser to reshape the cornea however, the method of surgery differed a bit from LASIK . During this surgery, the outer cells of the cornea are removed while keeping your eyes still by making you focus on a target light.
  • Small Incision Lenticule Extraction (SMILE)
    A minute incision is made on the surface of the cornea and it is reshaped using a laser. The incision heals on its own in a few days.

Can long hours of phone and computer usage damage the eyes?

Yes, long hours of exposure to phone and computer screens exposes your eyes to harmful blue light which causes strain and damage.

What happens if eye disorders are left untreated?

If eye disorders are left untreated then there is a chance of severe damage to the eyes resulting in permanent loss of vision.

What is the chance of recurrence of eye disorders after a FEMTO LASIK surgery?

There is a minimal chance of the recurrence of eye disorders after a FEMTO LASIK Surgery. This is only if the power of your eyes changes after the surgery, which is rare.

When can I get back to work after LASIK surgery?

You can get back to work within 2-3 days after a LASIK surgery.
